About Marcus Lundblad, a self biography

Dear visitor,

In my various roles, I have learned that security shall not be “yet another burden”. Instead it shall be designed around the well-known KISS-principle noted by the U.S Navy in 1960. The KISS principle states that most systems work best if they are kept simple rather then made heavily complicated; therefore, simplicity is an important cornerstone in my security solutions and well connected to any business needs and way of work.

I have had the great fortune to be involved with nearly every dimension of cyber security at international level during my career as a cyber security specialist. Some examples follow:

- Leading and executing penetration tests.
- Developed vulnerability management solutions and processes.
- Developed exploitation techniques and software.
- Preventing fraud and cyber incidents.
- Participated in agreement / outsourcing audits.
- Participated in implementing well known security standards
- Participated in implementing the data protection and communication laws.
- Participated in implementing the Protective Security Act.
- Participated in implementing the next generation of secure mobile communication.
- Participated in national security forums for critical infrastructure.
- Participated in international security projects.

Trained by:

- Swedish armed forces in a yearly red and blue team exercise and total defense strategy.
- Swedish civil contingencies agency in network monitoring and investigations.
- Supervisory authorities in national threats and total defense strategy within specific fields.
- International security researchers in writing exploit software.

I hope to have enlightened you a little bit about my career path and that we could speak soon.
